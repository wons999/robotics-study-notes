## One-line Summary

SaTA anchors tactile features in the hand's kinematic frame so policies can reason about contact geometry for high-precision dexterous manipulation.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: dexterous hand manipulation setup.
- Observation: tactile sensor signals spatially grounded through hand kinematics.
- Action: dexterous manipulation actions.
- Data: demonstrations for precision contact tasks.
- Task: sub-millimeter and contact-sensitive tasks such as USB-C mating, light bulb installation, and card sliding.

## Core Idea

Raw tactile observations are informative but not automatically geometrically meaningful. SaTA uses forward kinematics to anchor tactile features to the hand coordinate frame, allowing the policy to infer object geometry and contact relationships more directly.

## Method Details

- Architecture: end-to-end policy with spatially anchored tactile representations.
- Training recipe: train on precision dexterous manipulation tasks.
- Data: tactile-rich task data.
- Inference / control loop: policy reasons over contact signals grounded to hand geometry.

## What I Should Remember

- Tactile representation should preserve both sensory richness and spatial relation to the hand.
- T-Rex uses deformation maps and force histories; SaTA highlights the importance of kinematic grounding.
- This paper is important for thinking about tactile representation beyond feature fusion.

## Limitations

- Requires accurate kinematic calibration and sensor placement.
- It is not a VLA foundation model.
- Transfer across hands may require rethinking the tactile frame representation.
