## One-line Summary

EgoScale studies whether large-scale egocentric human video can provide a predictable and transferable motor prior for dexterous robotic manipulation.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: high-DoF dexterous hand and transfer to lower-DoF hands.
- Observation: egocentric human video during pretraining, robot observations during alignment and downstream manipulation.
- Action: action-labeled human motion and robot-executable dexterous actions.
- Data: more than 20,000 hours of action-labeled egocentric human video, then aligned robot mid-training.
- Task: long-horizon dexterous manipulation and one-shot task adaptation.

## Core Idea

EgoScale is built around the hypothesis that human egocentric video is a scalable source of physical intelligence for dexterous manipulation. The paper trains a VLA on very large human video data, then aligns it to robot execution through a simpler two-stage transfer recipe.

## Method Details

- Architecture: VLA-style model trained from egocentric human action data and adapted to robot control.
- Training recipe: large-scale human pretraining followed by aligned human-robot mid-training.
- Data: large egocentric video corpus with action labels.
- Inference / control loop: dexterous robot policy transferred from the human-derived motor prior.

## What I Should Remember

- T-Rex inherits the idea that human egocentric pretraining can provide broad visuomotor priors.
- T-Rex adds tactile-grounded mid-training and a tactile expert because EgoScale alone struggles with precise contact adjustment.
- EgoScale is a key paper for understanding the data-scaling side of T-Rex.

## Limitations

- Human video pretraining alone does not fully ground tactile contact dynamics.
- Egocentric video provides rich priors but not direct robot force/tactile feedback.
- Transfer still requires robot alignment data.
