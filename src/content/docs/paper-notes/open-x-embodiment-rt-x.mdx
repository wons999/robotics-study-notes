## One-line Summary

Open X-Embodiment standardizes and aggregates robot manipulation datasets across many robots, enabling cross-robot policy training and RT-X models.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: 22 different robots from a multi-institution collaboration.
- Observation: robot observations in standardized formats.
- Action: robot demonstrations across many skills and embodiments.
- Data: large-scale multi-robot dataset spanning hundreds of skills and many tasks.
- Task: train generalist policies that transfer across robots and downstream tasks.

## Core Idea

Robotics historically trains separate policies for each robot and task. Open X-Embodiment asks whether robotics can move toward the consolidation seen in NLP and vision: large models trained on diverse data, then adapted.

## Method Details

- Architecture: RT-X models trained over multi-robot data.
- Training recipe: standardize data and train high-capacity policies across embodiments.
- Data: demonstrations from many robots and institutions.
- Inference / control loop: cross-robot policy transfer and positive transfer analysis.

## What I Should Remember

- This is a data infrastructure paper as much as a model paper.
- OpenVLA and many later VLA papers rely on this scaling direction.
- T-Rex differs by focusing on tactile-rich dexterous bimanual data, not broad robot data alone.

## Limitations

- Cross-embodiment data does not automatically include rich tactile signals.
- Standardization across robots is hard, especially for high-DoF hands.
- Generality can hide gaps in contact-rich control.
