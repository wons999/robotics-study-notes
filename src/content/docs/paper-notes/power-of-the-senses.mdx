## One-line Summary

M3L jointly learns visual-tactile representations and policies through masked multimodal learning, improving sample efficiency and generalization in manipulation.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: simulated manipulation environments with visual and tactile observations.
- Observation: vision and touch.
- Action: RL policy actions.
- Data: simulated interaction data.
- Task: insertion, door opening, and dexterous in-hand manipulation.

## Core Idea

The paper uses masked multimodal learning to force the model to learn correspondences between vision and touch. This creates representations that are useful for policies and can even improve vision-only behavior at test time.

## Method Details

- Architecture: masked multimodal representation learner plus policy.
- Training recipe: jointly learn policy and visual-tactile representations.
- Data: simulated environments with paired visual and tactile observations.
- Inference / control loop: policy uses learned multimodal representations.

## What I Should Remember

- Vision and touch are complementary; masking helps learn cross-modal structure.
- This is representation-centric, whereas T-Rex is foundation-model and control-loop-centric.
- Tactile data can improve generalization even when not all modalities are available at test time.

## Limitations

- Experiments are simulation-focused.
- It does not address VLA-scale pretraining.
- It does not directly solve high-frequency real-world tactile control.
