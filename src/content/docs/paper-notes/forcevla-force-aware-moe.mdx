## One-line Summary

ForceVLA treats force feedback as a first-class modality in a VLA and uses a force-aware mixture-of-experts module to improve contact-rich manipulation.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: contact-rich manipulation platform with force sensing.
- Observation: vision, proprioception, and six-axis force-torque signals.
- Action: VLA-style action decoding enhanced by force-aware routing.
- Data: ForceVLA-Data, synchronized multimodal data across contact-rich tasks.
- Task: manipulation tasks such as insertion where force feedback matters.

## Core Idea

ForceVLA argues that contact-rich tasks need explicit force-aware routing, not just visual-language embeddings. Its FVLMoE module dynamically integrates force feedback with pretrained visual-language features during action decoding.

## Method Details

- Architecture: force-aware Mixture-of-Experts fusion module.
- Training recipe: train VLA with force-torque signals and contact-rich task data.
- Data: synchronized vision, proprioception, and force-torque demonstrations.
- Inference / control loop: action decoder uses real-time force features.

## What I Should Remember

- ForceVLA and T-Rex both elevate physical feedback into the VLA architecture.
- ForceVLA focuses on force-aware MoE fusion; T-Rex focuses on high-frequency tactile refinement and tactile deformation/force history.
- Both are responses to the same weakness: visual VLAs struggle in contact.

## Limitations

- Force sensing is not identical to dense tactile sensing.
- Generalization depends on the diversity of contact-rich force data.
- Timing and high-frequency control are less central than in T-Rex.
