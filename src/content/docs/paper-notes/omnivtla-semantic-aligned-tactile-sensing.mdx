## One-line Summary

OmniVTLA adds semantic-aligned tactile sensing to VLA policies through dual tactile encoders and an ObjTac dataset for tactile-visual-language alignment.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: gripper and dexterous-hand manipulation platforms.
- Observation: visual input, language instruction, and tactile signals.
- Action: VLA-generated manipulation actions.
- Data: ObjTac, a tri-modal tactile dataset with force-based tactile readings, text, and visual data.
- Task: pick-and-place and manipulation tasks where tactile sensing improves smoothness and success.

## Core Idea

The paper focuses on heterogeneous tactile sensors. It proposes a dual-path tactile encoder framework: one path uses pretrained vision transformer features, and the other uses a semantically aligned tactile ViT. This attempts to make tactile representations more compatible with VLA semantic spaces.

## Method Details

- Architecture: dual-path tactile encoder with pretrained ViT and SA-ViT.
- Training recipe: train semantic tactile alignment on ObjTac, then use it as initialization for VLA manipulation.
- Data: ObjTac with force-based tactile, visual, and textual information.
- Inference / control loop: tactile-enhanced VLA policy for manipulation.

## What I Should Remember

- OmniVTLA is about semantic tactile representation and sensor heterogeneity.
- T-Rex is about high-frequency tactile-reactive refinement and dexterous bimanual control.
- Both show that tactile needs dedicated representation design, not only raw concatenation.

## Limitations

- Semantic alignment alone may not provide high-frequency reactivity.
- Dataset and sensor coverage determine transfer.
- Contact-rich dexterity can still require temporal tactile modeling.
