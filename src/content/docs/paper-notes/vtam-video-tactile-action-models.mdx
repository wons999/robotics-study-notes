## One-line Summary

VTAM augments video-action models with tactile streams so action models can correct visual estimation errors in contact-rich physical interaction.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: contact-rich manipulation setup.
- Observation: video streams plus tactile feedback.
- Action: action predictions from a video-tactile action model.
- Data: contact-rich manipulation data with tactile feedback.
- Task: physical interaction tasks where visual tokens alone miss force and contact transitions.

## Core Idea

Video-action models can model temporal visual dynamics, but contact state is often partially or fully invisible. VTAM adds tactile grounding to pretrained video transformers, using lightweight modality-transfer finetuning and a tactile regularization loss to prevent the model from ignoring touch.

## Method Details

- Architecture: pretrained video transformer augmented with tactile streams.
- Training recipe: modality-transfer finetuning plus tactile regularization for balanced cross-modal attention.
- Data: video-tactile-action data for contact-rich tasks.
- Inference / control loop: action prediction grounded by video and tactile observations.

## What I Should Remember

- VTAM focuses on video-action/world-model style policies rather than language-first VLA policies.
- It supports the same thesis as T-Rex: visual dynamics are not enough for contact.
- T-Rex's tactile expert can be read as a control-frequency answer to the same physical grounding problem.

## Limitations

- It may not provide the same language-conditioned generality as VLA systems.
- Tactile fusion quality depends on attention balance and data diversity.
- It is still tied to the tactile sensing setup used in training.
