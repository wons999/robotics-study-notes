## One-line Summary

Tactile-VLA connects tactile sensing to a VLA so the model can use physical interaction knowledge for contact-rich instruction following and adaptive behavior.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: robot manipulation platform with tactile sensing.
- Observation: vision, language, action history, and tactile information.
- Action: VLA-generated action interpreted through a hybrid position-force controller.
- Data: a small number of demonstrations for tactile grounding.
- Task: tactile-aware instruction following, contact-rich reasoning, and adaptive manipulation.

## Core Idea

The paper argues that VLM/VLA backbones already contain semantic knowledge about physical interactions, but this knowledge is not grounded in tactile execution. The method fuses tactile sensing into the policy and uses a hybrid position-force controller to turn model intent into contact-sensitive action.

## Method Details

- Architecture: vision-language-action model with tactile fusion and a tactile reasoning module.
- Training recipe: connect tactile inputs to a pretrained model with limited demonstrations.
- Data: tactile demonstrations for grounding physical concepts.
- Inference / control loop: model uses tactile feedback to adapt strategy and force-position behavior.

## What I Should Remember

- Tactile-VLA is about activating physical commonsense in a VLA through touch.
- It emphasizes high-level tactile reasoning and hybrid control.
- T-Rex finds that simply adding tactile signals to pretrained VLA-style policies is not enough; architecture and timing matter.

## Limitations

- The paper is less focused on high-frequency tactile-reactive control than T-Rex.
- Generality depends on how well tactile signals are aligned with the pretrained VLA.
- Hardware and sensor assumptions can affect transfer.
