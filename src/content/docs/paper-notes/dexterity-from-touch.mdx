## One-line Summary

T-Dex learns tactile encoders from self-supervised robotic play, then uses those representations with a small number of demonstrations for dexterous tasks.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: multi-fingered robot with tactile sensing.
- Observation: tactile readings plus visual observations.
- Action: policies for dexterous manipulation.
- Data: play data for tactile pretraining and a few demonstrations for target tasks.
- Task: fine-grained dexterous manipulation where vision or torque alone is insufficient.

## Core Idea

The paper separates tactile representation learning from task policy learning. First, collect broad tactile play data and train encoders without task labels. Then use the learned tactile embedding for downstream dexterous policies.

## Method Details

- Architecture: self-supervised tactile encoder plus non-parametric downstream policies.
- Training recipe: pretrain on tactile play, then learn task behavior from limited demonstrations.
- Data: 2.5 hours of play data plus few-shot demonstrations.
- Inference / control loop: policy combines tactile and visual observations.

## What I Should Remember

- Tactile representation learning can be data-efficient if pretraining uses broad play.
- This is an early signal that touch is especially useful under occlusion and fine contact.
- T-Rex scales the idea from tactile pretraining into tactile-grounded VLA mid-training.

## Limitations

- Dataset and policy scale are much smaller than VLA foundation-model papers.
- Non-parametric downstream policies may not scale to broad task families.
- Hardware-specific tactile data can limit transfer.
