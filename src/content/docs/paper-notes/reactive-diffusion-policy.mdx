## One-line Summary

RDP combines a slow latent diffusion policy with a fast tactile-conditioned controller so a robot can react to contact feedback during execution rather than waiting for the next visual action chunk.

## Problem Setting

- Robot embodiment: contact-rich manipulation platform with visual and tactile or force sensing.
- Observation: visual input plus real-time tactile or force feedback.
- Action: action chunks modeled by a latent diffusion policy, then adjusted through a fast tactile pathway.
- Data: demonstrations collected with TactAR, a teleoperation system that gives operators tactile feedback through augmented reality.
- Task: contact-rich manipulation requiring quick force/contact adjustment.

## Core Idea

Many imitation-learning policies predict action chunks. That helps model long-horizon behavior but creates a latency problem: if tactile feedback changes midway through the chunk, the policy may not react quickly enough.

RDP addresses this with a two-level hierarchy:

- Slow latent diffusion policy: predicts high-level action chunks at low frequency.
- Fast asymmetric tokenizer: updates behavior at higher frequency using tactile feedback.

This is a direct conceptual ancestor for T-Rex's slow visual stream plus fast tactile stream.

## Method Details

- Architecture: slow latent diffusion policy plus fast tactile-conditioned tokenizer.
- Training recipe: train a tactile-conditioned action tokenizer, then train latent diffusion over the learned action space.
- Data: contact-rich demonstrations collected with tactile feedback in the teleoperation loop.
- Inference / control loop: low-frequency diffusion trajectory generation with high-frequency tactile correction.

## What I Should Remember

- RDP frames tactile feedback as an execution-time reactivity problem.
- It is task-specific imitation learning, not a large VLA foundation model.
- T-Rex scales a similar slow-fast idea into a VLA/MoT framework and broader dexterous bimanual tasks.

## Limitations

- It is not designed as a generalist VLA.
- The reported task scope is narrower than T-Rex.
- It still depends on collecting contact-rich demonstrations.
